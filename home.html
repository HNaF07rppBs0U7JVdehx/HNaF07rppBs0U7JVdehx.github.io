CBDC Essay
The Barter System started back in 6000 BCE. Humans are not self-sufficient. To solve this, they would trade directly with one another, exchanging specific goods (for example, leather for crops).
Commodity Money addressed many problems the barter system possessed. Standard of value was established when valued items such as salt, cattle, and tea was agreed on to be valuable currency. Itâ€™s disputable when they first emerged, but it was before 1000 BC, when the first coins were created.
Metallic Money was able to fix a major problem of its predecessors. Storage. The earliest use of coins was in China from 1000 BC. One problem, however, was that the coin productions were often centralized, the head of state controlling the system often led to inflation in many cases as new coins were created to often repay debt from war.
Paper Money, which first appeared in the 11th century was invented by the Chinese, and was easy to store. But, it still could not fix the problem of over supply. Almost all countries have made one form of paper money their fiat money, or government-backed with trust.
Plastic Money arrived in the late 20th century. ATM cards, debit cards, credit cards, are all forms of plastic money. Now at this point, it was even a hassle to carry money around! or there was fear of losing money by theft. With the age of internet, plastic money is crucial in internet transactions.
Digital Currencies arrived in the 21st century (e-gold, a virtual currency attempt was in 1996). However, it was the Bitcoin, created by a certain Satoshi Nakomoto (a pseudonym) who was able to solve the double-spend problem using blockchain. The double-spend problem refers to the problem with digital currencies which in some cases a user was about spend multiple times using the same file or medium on which the currency is operating in.
